Review of Behave: The Biology of Humans at Our Best and Worst by Robert M. Sapolsky

The Book

This book shall be of particular interest to us as it does a good job at defining human nature in scientific and relatively objective terms that generally allign with the objectives of our organization.

Sapolsky recounts himself as a young person fantasizing about torturing and killing Hitler.

Sapolsky expresses a personal fear and repulsion to violence, but describes a society that in so many ways worships it.
