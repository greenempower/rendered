GEM Brief: Libre, Open Source, Creative Commons

The libre software movement was led pioneered by Richard Stallman and hi's Free Software Foundation.

With his work, persona and instantiation of the GPL License he has positioned himself at the more radical end of the larger open source movement.

His emphasis is on user freedom which can encroach on the aims of corporations.

But as many have realized he is essentially a rad-lib and a reformer rather than a revolutionary marxist.

Now Open Source and Creative Commons are neoliberal. They strengthen and server capitalism without fighting it in any meaningful way.

They do this by improving efficiency of corporations in the market by implementing a shared commons which is still meant for exploitation by those companies rather than a fair commons by which every individual benefits and contributes without outsized exploitation by capitalist parasites.

I am now using the Peer Production License detailed in the The Telekommunist Manifesto.

It is a solid ideological basis but I am unsure of the specific emphasis on cooperatives. And I am pessimistic that this license will realistically be enforced in this current stage of neoliberal extraction.

Generally our ideology needs a deeper material basis, by that I mean economic and military power. China and the global south grows but here in the imperial core we are subject to the brutal fist of the parasites.
